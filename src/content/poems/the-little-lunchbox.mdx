morning comes in early,
pulling you out of my warm hands
you slip into the day,
doing the necessary things —
the grown-up, world-holding, bread-winning things,
that pull you from me
while i stay here,
trying not to fold in half
from how much i want you near.

i pack a warm little lunchbox for you:
sunlight on my face,
my coffee accidentally frothing a heart,
the flowers at the stall,
my lunch, my thoughts, my small joys.

i save them all for you,
tucked like candies in my pockets,
soft around the edges
from being held too tight.
and oh, it's foolish,
so silly, this embarrassing childlike ache.
but God,
my whole day feels too big,
too loud, too crowded
without you to tell it to,
without having your warm face to look at
again, and again,
all the time
