the ghost of your hand still lingers on my shoulders
it traces my collarbones in the shape of a clock–
hours slipping through my skin, while I lie
awake and dead, staring at the ceiling

and the echoes of your voice hum in the hollow of my ribs,
like a song you never bothered to finish—
haunting me in the silences between my breaths,
stealing air from my lungs, choking me.

i feel you in the spaces –
  in the shadows of my spine,
  in the curves of my hips;
you now haunt me in the places you kissed

I close my eyes and you are always kissing my wounds,
like a long-lost lover, desperate to taste what's left
I close my eyes and you are feeding on my soul,
like a starving man, devouring like it's his last meal

how long will I stand at your closed door, still yearning?
how long will I be the map of your ghost, still haunting?
