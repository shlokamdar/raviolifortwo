			after Frank O'Hara 

is more fun than rotting in my room, watching modern family for the 7th time
or going to a club, on a friday night, glitter in my hair, feet aching and dancing.

having an ice-cream with you, on a random thursday night, sitting at the sidewalk
is even more fun than going to a fancy ice cream parlour–

partly because you listen to me ramble about another failed situationship,
			         that left me craving ice-cream to numb the pain.
partly because of the way we laugh and all the sadness melts before the ice cream
        starts dripping from the cone.
partly because of the way the yellow street lights shine and look like mangoes
			        that somehow found their way to this scoop in my hand.

it is hard to believe that when i'm having a large scoop of mango icecream with you,
i forget how fast the world is actually spinning. everything softens–
as if time itself has paused and sat down to hear us plot our next crazy adventure
where we sneak out without telling our parents.
as if the hands of the clock fell down to taste the mango ice cream
we had that night, sitting at the sidewalk–
	because, having an ice-cream with you is fun.

like being girls together, giggling under the streetlight
whispering secrets on a summer night,

like tasting selfless love in a cone and on your shoulder again.
