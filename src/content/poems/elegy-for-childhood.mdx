I remember the summer evening of when you were seven,
the air carried a gentle breeze
you lost your first milk teeth, a gap-toothed, infectious grin.

your laughter wiggled its way out and turned every frown
in the house upside-down
you were a kid  and happy
and didn't know what it meant to drown
and all you wanted to do was,
dance and pluck all the flowers to make a crown.

the sand castles you built were taller than you,
you played hide and seek in summer's sun
and somehow your laugh echoed
always behind the yellow curtains.

afternoons stretched its arms and you dreamt on the grass,
wanting to touch the sky  only to eat the cotton candy clouds.

but time was a river, it kept flowing
and dried up before you could remember
the feeling of cool water on your toes.
oh to be seven again, swimming and dancing
like a happy fish, free in the cold stream
oh, to build sand castles
and to wake up with a toothless grin, in this dream
